Domain 2 Definitions
  1. Confidentiality - prevents the unauthorized disclosure of data
  2. Encryption - scrambles data to make it unreadable by unauthorized personnel
  3. Identification - Users claim identity with unique username
  4. Authentication - prove identity with password or something else
  5. Authorization - grant or restrict access to resources after authentication
  6. Availability - Data and services are available when needed
  7. Steganography - hiding data within data
  8. Integrity - ensures the data hasn't been modified/tampered with - 2 key concepts
    • Provides assurance that data has not been modified, tampered with, or corrupted
    • Hashing verifies ingrity
  9. Hashing - used to enforce integrity. As long as two documents are the same the hash will not change
  10. Hash - a number created by executing a hashing algorithm against data
  11. Redundancy - Goal to provide fault tolerance and to remove a single point of failure
  12. Patching - updates to software to stop known exploits and correct bugs
  13. Layered Security/Defense in Depth - security practice of implementing several layers of protection
  14. Controls - how someone controls risk
  15. Risk - likelihood a threat will exploit a vulnerability resulting in a loss
  16. Risk mitigation - uses controls to reduce risk
  17. Security incident - adverse event or series of events that can negatively affect the CIA of an organizations IT systems and data
  18. Technical controls - use technology to reduce vulnerabilities
  19. Management controls - use planning and methods to reduce and manage risk
  20. Risk assessments - help quantify and qualify risk in an org so the org can focus only on serious risks
  21. Vulnerability assessments - attempts to disvoer current vulnerabilities/weaknesses
  22. Penetration tests - go beyond vulnerability assessment by attempting to exploit vulnerabilities
  23. Operational controls - Help ensure that day-to-day operations of an organization comply with overall security plan
  24. Secuirty audit - determine the security posture of an organization
  25. Active IDS (intrusion detection system) - an IDS that can also modify the enviroment to block the attack from continuing
  26. Deterent controls - attempt ot discourage a threat
  27. Compensation controls - alternative controls used instead of a primary control
  28. Cipher locks - pattern based door locking mechanism. Can be electronic or manual
  29. Proximity cards - RFID cards that unlock a door when they trigger a sensor
  30. Tailgating - someone follows closely behind someone else to get through physical security; prevented with mantraps
  31. Mantraps - mechanism designed to control access to a securie area through a buffer zone - only allows one person through at a time
  32. Bollards - waist high steel and concrete posts that prevent cars from driving through a wall to break into a building
  33. False positive - an alert/alarm triggered when an event is non-threatening
  34. False negative - a threatening event that does not trigger an alert/alarm
  35. Honeypot - a "sweet" looking server that is left open and encourages an attacker to attack with relative ease. Tricks them into thinking they have accessed data but nothing is compromised. Also helpful to learn how an attacker works
  36. Honeynet - basically a honeypot but an entire network
  37. File-level encryption - encryption of a file or folder, most OS have built in tools to acomplish
  38. Data exfiltration - the unauthorized transfer of data outside an organization
  39. DLP (Data Loss Prevention) - examine and inspect data looking for unauthorized data transmissions
  40. SAN (Storage Area Network) - provides connectivity for high-speed data storage devices in specialized high-speed network
  41. SaS (Software as a Service) - application/software provided for users over the internet or other network
  42. Threat Vector - the method used to activate a threat and cna originate from three primary sources (external, internal, and the supply chain)
  43. Malicious insider - anyone who has legitimate access to an organizations internal resources but exploits this access for personal gain or damage agains the organization
  44. Risk management - the practice of identifying, monitoring, and limiting risks to a manageable level
  45. Quantatative risk assessment - measures the risk using specific monetary ammount
  46. Qualitative measurements - use judgements to determine what is acceptable risk
  47. Vulnerability Assessment - identify vulnerabilities, or weaknesses, within systems, networks, and organizations, part of overall risk management plan
  48. Passive testing of security controls - does not attempt to exploit any vulnerabilities
  49. User review - review permissions of users
  50. Five Nines - 99.999% uptime. Equates to less than 6 minutes of downtime a year
  51. Security policies - written documents that lay out a security plan within a company
  52. On-boarding - granting someone access to a system
  53. Off-boarding - reversing the process of on-boarding
  54. Storage and retention policy - where data is stored and how long it is retained
  55. Chain of custody - provides assurance that evicence has been controlled and handled properly after collection; documents all who and why evidence is handled

Domain 6
  • Digital signature - an encrypted hash of a message (encrypted with the sender's private key)
  • Cipher suite - combination of cryptographic algorithms that provide several layers of secuirty for TLS and SSL
  • Key stretching - technique used to increase the strenght of stored passwords
  • PKI (Public Key Infrastructure) - Group of technologies used to request, create, manage, store, distribute, and revoke digital certificates
